Privacy Policy

TimeTrack + Estimates & the TimeTrack Timer Chrome extension · last updated 20 July 2026

TimeTrack does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal data. There is no account, no server, and no analytics. Everything you enter stays on your own device.

What is stored, and where

  • The web app stores your projects, time entries, estimates, invoices and settings in your browser's localStorage, on your device only.
  • The Chrome extension stores the running timer, tracked time and your task list in chrome.storage.local, on your device only.

None of this data ever leaves your computer. It is not sent to us or to any third party.

How the extension uses its permissions

  • storage — to remember the running timer and tracked time between browser sessions.
  • alarms — to update the elapsed-time badge on the toolbar icon while a timer runs.
  • Access to the TimeTrack app page — so tracked time can be written into the app you have open. The extension only acts on a page that identifies itself as the TimeTrack app; it does not read or touch any other website.

Data deletion

Because data is stored only in your browser, you are in full control: clear the app's data from within the app, remove the extension, or clear your browser storage at any time.
